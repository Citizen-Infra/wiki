---
title: "Community Supplies: a sharing app that spread to 200 neighborhoods"
description: One friend's leftover party supplies became a free tool for neighbours to share what they own — and after a newspaper column, hundreds of neighbourhoods started their own overnight.
---

It started at a kid's birthday party. Josh Nesbit's friend MJ had bought a pile of party supplies and didn't want them to just sit in his garage — "they should be shared with people here instead." That night, the two built the first version of a neighbourhood **sharing app**. The response was immediate: *"Holy [shoot], you built it?"* — followed at once by *"what if it did this?"*

The instinct it answered is a familiar one: **"I do not want my neighbour going to Home Depot to buy the tool I have in my garage."** Big platforms had never really solved it; a small, local, co-built tool could.

## From one block to 200 neighbourhoods

The app — **[Community Supplies](https://communitysupplies.org/)** (free and open-source) — lets neighbours photograph what they're willing to lend, set simple lending guidelines, and borrow from each other. When MJ's experience was written up in a *Washington Post* column ("I wanted to buy less stuff. I ended up bonding with my neighbors," 2026), the makers turned the app **multi-tenant** the morning it ran — so that anyone could start their own. Over the next three days, **about 200 neighbourhoods** launched their own sharing communities.

## Why it's a story worth knowing

Community Supplies is the clearest example of [locality-to-locality spreading](/concepts/locality-to-locality-spreading/): not one platform scaling to millions, but a small tool *remixed* by community after community. As Josh found, the technology was almost a "Trojan horse" — neighbours told him it had simply given them *permission* to ask one another, "are we okay sharing things with each other?" That permission, more than the app, is the [relational tech](/concepts/relational-tech/). It was built through the [Relational Technology Project](/ecosystem/overview/).
