This is a **free, public resource**. You don't need to join anything or sign up to read it or use what's here. It's for any citizen who wants to understand citizen infrastructure — and find tools they can actually use.

## What's here

- **[The idea](/idea/technology-for-collective-action/)** — what citizen infrastructure is and the thinking behind it, including the *Digital Pitchforks* manifesto.
- **[The toolkit](/toolkit/overview/)** — tools grouped by what you want to do: connect with neighbours, coordinate a community, decide together, and more. *(Being filled in — more tools land here over time.)*
- **[Library](/library/reading-and-sources/)** — the deeper reading: white papers and sources worth your time. *(Also growing.)*

## Who's behind it

This resource is stewarded by the **Citizen Infrastructure Builders Club (CIBC)** — but it's about the idea and the tools, not the organisation. If you want to join or support the club, the [About page](/about/) has the links.

It's all open. Spot something wrong or missing? Use the **"Edit page"** link at the bottom of any page, or browse the source [on GitHub](https://github.com/Citizen-Infra/wiki).
