There are thousands of civic initiatives — and they mostly operate in disconnected silos, precisely when coordinated responses are most needed. **Civic intelligence infrastructure** (CII), a concept developed by Brandon Nørgaard for the [Mediators Foundation](/ecosystem/overview/) with [Better Together America](/ecosystem/overview/), is a proposed answer: the *connective tissue* between civic tools, and an intelligence layer that makes their combined output legible.

## The problems it names

- **Invisibility** — groups doing complementary work never discover each other, even a few miles apart.
- **Coordination failure** — no easy way to find collaborators across geographic or functional boundaries.
- **Fragmented infrastructure** — every organisation runs separate event, CRM, and measurement tools that can't talk to each other.
- **Impact opacity** — the most important outcomes (relationships, trust, systemic change) go unmeasured.
- **Knowledge fragmentation** — each hub reinvents what another already solved.
- **Systems blindness** — groups treat symptoms without seeing the system they're part of.

## The idea: keep your tools, gain a shared backend

CII proposes two layers. A **normalization layer** translates the data from each group's existing tools into a shared "civic data taxonomy" — so coordinators keep the tools they already use, and a common standard runs underneath. On top of that sits a **backend intelligence layer**: ecosystem mapping (organisations, relationships, resource flows, gaps) and aggregate analytics that make scattered local action legible as one coordinated movement — for network coordinators, funders, and researchers. It's meant to *integrate* existing civic tech, not replace it.

## Why not just point an AI at it?

High-stakes civic analysis needs rigour a pure-chatbot approach can't guarantee. CII's design keeps a **knowledge graph** as the source of truth — defining what counts as valid and tracing every claim back to the instrument and organisation it came from — and uses language models only at the edges (natural-language data entry, drafting reports). The goal is provenance and consistency over a confident black box. It also normalises impact across many levels at once — individual skills, relationships and trust, behaviour, systems change, community outcomes — the holistic picture funders increasingly ask for.

It's an early-stage, in-development concept; Better Together America's network of civic hubs is the intended beta. Think of it as a concrete proposal for the "intelligence layer" gap described in [connecting the tools](/concepts/interoperability-and-standards/), and a cousin of the questions raised in [AI for participation](/concepts/ai-for-participation/).
