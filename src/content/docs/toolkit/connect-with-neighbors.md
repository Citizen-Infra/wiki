title: Connect with your neighbors
description: Tools that help you meet the people on your street and start caring for each other — the first, smallest layer of citizen infrastructure.
---

The smallest, most overlooked piece of citizen infrastructure is also the first: simply **knowing the people who live around you**. These tools help you make the introduction and start small acts of mutual care — the spirit of [relational tech](/concepts/relational-tech/).

- **[Relational Tech Studio](https://relationaltechproject.org/)** (Relational Technology Project) — a chat-driven tool that helps you start a neighbour tool of your own. You tell it about your street and its needs, and it helps you **remix** an existing example into something for your block (often in minutes), with a build plan to follow. It draws on a shared commons of hundreds of real neighbouring projects.
- **[Community Supplies](https://communitysupplies.org/)** — a free, open-source app for neighbours to **share tools, supplies, and party gear** instead of everyone buying their own. Start one for your street, or join an existing sharing community. (See the [Story](/stories/community-supplies/).)
- **[Neighbor-gathering microgrants](https://withneighbors.org/)** (Relational Tech Project & Connective Tissue) — small grants (around $100) that act as "a permission slip to invite your neighbours" — enough to cover the coffee, donuts, or flyers for a first gathering.

A surprising amount starts with something as simple as a flyer and a pot of coffee in a driveway — see [The Outer Sunset](/stories/outer-sunset/).
