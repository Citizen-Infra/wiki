---
title: Coordinate a group or community
description: Tools that help a community organise itself — set its own rules, roles, and ways of making decisions.
---

Before a group can act together, it has to organise itself: agree on who decides what, how, and by what rules. Most online communities never get this far — they default to one all-powerful admin (what Nathan Schneider calls [implicit feudalism](/concepts/implicit-feudalism/)). These tools help a group do better, by designing its own governance deliberately.

- **[CommunityRule](https://communityrule.info/)** (Media Economies Design Lab) — a simple drag-and-drop web app for designing, editing, and sharing your community's own governance: its norms for decision-making, stewardship, and culture. It ships with templates, so a group can start from a known pattern rather than a blank page — or the default of handing one person all the power.
