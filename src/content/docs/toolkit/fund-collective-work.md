---
title: Fund collective work
description: Tools and models for raising money for shared, community-owned work — without the funding pulling the work away from its mission.
---

Shared work needs shared resources. These tools help a community raise and manage money for collective work — keeping it transparent, and keeping the core a commons rather than a product. For the bigger picture of *how* commons-based projects get funded, see [Funding the commons](/concepts/funding-the-commons/).

## Raise money together

- **[Goteo](https://www.goteo.org/)** — a crowdfunding platform built specifically for the commons and community-oriented projects, which can add institutional *match-funding* on top of community donations.
- **[Open Collective](https://opencollective.com/)** — transparent collective finances: every contribution and expense is public, and a "fiscal host" handles the legal entity and banking, so a group without its own legal structure can still receive and spend money openly.

## Keep it going

- **[Liberapay](https://liberapay.com/)** — recurring donations to people and projects, run as a non-profit with no cut taken; good for sustaining ongoing work rather than one-off campaigns.

## Decide together how to spend it

- **[CoBudget](https://cobudget.com/)** — lets a community allocate a shared pot of money *collaboratively*: members propose ideas, see the budget, and decide together where it goes. Pairs naturally with participatory budgeting.

Two patterns worth knowing as you grow — **capped returns** (investors earn up to a ceiling, then the venture reinvests all surplus in its mission) and **open value accounting** (tracking and rewarding everyone who contributes, not just founders) — are explained in [Funding the commons](/concepts/funding-the-commons/).
